## Band pressdown

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Band pressdown

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Forearms

**4. Equipment:** Resistance band

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Letting elbows drift forward
  - Using shoulder movement instead of elbow extension
  - Snapping the band back up
  - Leaning over excessively
  - Using an unstable anchor

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Check the band and anchor before use.
  - Keep tension smooth and controlled.
  - Avoid locking elbows aggressively.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you press down. Inhale as you return.

**9. Tempo:** Press down for 1 second, return for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Elbows stay pinned
  - Press straight down
  - Return under control

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Single-arm light band pressdown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Elbows stay pinned".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use a stronger band.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use a stronger band
  - Add a pause at the bottom
  - Slow the return

**15. Regressions:**
  - Single-arm light band pressdown

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Bench dip
  - Close-grip push-up
  - Overhead dumbbell extension
  - Rope overhead extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The band pressdown trains the triceps by extending the elbows against band tension. It is a joint-friendly arm exercise that works well with a high anchor.

**Setup:** Anchor a resistance band overhead. Hold the band ends with elbows tucked at your sides, chest tall, and shoulders relaxed.

**Execution:** Press the hands downward by straightening the elbows. Keep upper arms close to the body and avoid swinging. Return slowly until elbows bend again.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand facing the anchor with elbows tucked, hands near lower chest height, and shoulders relaxed.
  - Mid: Press the band down by extending the elbows while keeping upper arms still.
  - Peak: Reach full triceps contraction with arms extended and wrists controlled.
  - Finish: Allow the elbows to bend slowly and return to the starting position under tension.

## Bench dip

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: compound

**1. Exercise name:** Bench dip

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Anterior deltoids, Chest

**4. Equipment:** Bench or sturdy elevated surface

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner to intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Letting shoulders roll forward
  - Moving hips too far away from the bench
  - Lowering too deep
  - Shrugging shoulders
  - Bouncing out of the bottom

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Avoid this movement if it causes front-shoulder pain.
  - Keep range conservative.
  - Use close control and do not force depth.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale as you lower. Exhale as you press up.

**9. Tempo:** Lower slowly for 2 seconds, press up with control.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Keep hips close
  - Chest stays open
  - Lower only as deep as shoulders tolerate

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Cable or band triceps pressdown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Keep hips close".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Straighten legs.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Straighten legs
  - Elevate feet only if shoulders tolerate it
  - Progress to close-grip push-up

**15. Regressions:**
  - Cable or band triceps pressdown
  - Close-grip wall push-up

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Close-grip push-up
  - Overhead dumbbell extension
  - Rope overhead extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The bench dip is a bodyweight triceps exercise performed with hands on a bench or sturdy surface. It can be effective but requires careful shoulder positioning.

**Setup:** Sit on the edge of a bench, place hands beside hips, and walk feet forward. Keep chest open, shoulders down, and hips close to the bench.

**Execution:** Bend the elbows to lower the body while keeping hips close to the bench. Lower only as far as shoulders feel comfortable. Press through the hands to extend elbows and return up.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Place hands on the bench beside your hips, walk feet forward, and keep hips close to the bench.
  - Mid: Bend the elbows and lower under control without letting shoulders roll forward.
  - Peak: Reach the lowest comfortable position while keeping chest open and shoulders controlled.
  - Finish: Press through the hands, extend the elbows, and return to the top without locking aggressively.

## Close-grip push-up

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: compound

**1. Exercise name:** Close-grip push-up

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps, Chest

**3. Secondary muscles:** Anterior deltoids, Core

**4. Equipment:** Bodyweight

**5. Difficulty:** Intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Setting hands so close that wrists or elbows hurt
  - Letting elbows flare out
  - Sagging the hips
  - Cutting range too short
  - Losing shoulder control

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use a comfortable hand width; hands do not need to touch.
  - Elevate hands if wrist, elbow, or shoulder stress is too high.
  - Stop before form breaks.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale as you lower. Exhale as you press up.

**9. Tempo:** Lower for 2 seconds, press up steadily.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Hands narrow but comfortable
  - Elbows brush the ribs
  - Move as one solid plank

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Incline close-grip push-up.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Hands narrow but comfortable".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Slow eccentric close-grip push-ups.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Slow eccentric close-grip push-ups
  - Paused reps
  - Deficit close-grip push-ups

**15. Regressions:**
  - Incline close-grip push-up
  - Band pressdown

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Bench dip
  - Deficit push-up
  - Overhead dumbbell extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The close-grip push-up is a push-up variation with the hands set narrower than a standard push-up. It increases triceps demand while still training the chest and shoulders.

**Setup:** Place hands under or slightly inside shoulder width. Set the body in a straight plank from head to heels, brace the core, and keep elbows close to the body.

**Execution:** Lower the body as one unit while elbows track back instead of flaring wide. Press the floor away and return to the top while keeping body alignment.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Set a narrow hand position, brace the core, and create a straight line from head to heels.
  - Mid: Lower with elbows tracking close to the ribs and shoulders controlled.
  - Peak: Reach the lowest controlled position without hips sagging or elbows flaring wide.
  - Finish: Press the floor away and return to a strong plank position.

## Cross-body triceps extension

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Cross-body triceps extension

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Shoulders

**4. Equipment:** Dumbbells

**5. Difficulty:** standard

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Rushing reps without control.
  - Letting posture break before the set ends.
  - Using momentum instead of tension.

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use a load and range you can control cleanly.
  - Stop the set when the rep stops matching the target pattern.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale on the controlled lowering phase and exhale as you drive through the main effort.

**9. Tempo:** Lift with control, squeeze briefly where it counts, and lower slower than you lift.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Move with control.
  - Keep tension where the exercise is meant to work.
  - Stay smooth from rep to rep.

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Use less weight or a smaller range of motion to start.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Move with control.".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Add a little weight or a few more reps as it gets easier.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** general full-body control — no special mobility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Add a little weight or a few more reps as it gets easier
  - Slow the lowering part or add a short pause to make it harder without more weight

**15. Regressions:**
  - Use less weight or a smaller range of motion to start
  - Do fewer reps and rest a little longer between sets

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Preacher curl
  - Reverse curl
  - Rope hammer curl
  - Rope overhead extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** Cross-body triceps extension is a single-arm triceps movement that lets you finish across the body under constant tension.

**Setup:** Set the handle for a single arm, brace the trunk, and start with the elbow tucked near the side of the body.

**Execution:** Extend the elbow across the body without twisting the torso, then return slowly until the forearm bends again.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Set the handle for one arm, tuck the elbow near the side, and square the trunk before you start.
  - Mid: Straighten the elbow across the body without letting the shoulder roll or the torso twist.
  - Peak: Squeeze briefly at full extension while the upper arm stays mostly still.
  - Finish: Return slowly until the elbow bends again and reset before the next rep.

## Overhead dumbbell extension

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Overhead dumbbell extension

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Shoulders, Core

**4. Equipment:** Dumbbell

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner to intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Flaring elbows too wide
  - Arching the low back
  - Letting the dumbbell drop too fast
  - Moving the shoulders instead of the elbows
  - Using too heavy a weight

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Keep range pain-free around elbows and shoulders.
  - Use a secure grip on the dumbbell.
  - Avoid forcing deep elbow flexion if it causes discomfort.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale as the dumbbell lowers. Exhale as you extend the elbows.

**9. Tempo:** Lower for 2-3 seconds, extend smoothly.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Elbows point forward
  - Upper arms stay quiet
  - Keep ribs stacked

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pressdown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Elbows point forward".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use a heavier dumbbell.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** shoulders and upper back that can reach overhead comfortably.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use a heavier dumbbell
  - Slow eccentric lowering
  - Single-arm overhead extension

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Close-grip push-up from an elevated surface

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Bench dip
  - Close-grip push-up
  - Rope overhead extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Shoulders: stay in a pain-free range and stop if you feel any pinching.
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The overhead dumbbell extension trains the triceps by extending the elbows while the arms are positioned overhead. It emphasizes the long head of the triceps.

**Setup:** Hold one dumbbell with both hands around the inside of the top plate or handle. Bring it overhead with elbows pointing forward and upper arms close to the head. Brace the core and keep ribs down.

**Execution:** Bend the elbows to lower the dumbbell behind the head. Keep upper arms mostly still. Extend the elbows to raise the dumbbell back overhead.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Hold the dumbbell overhead with elbows forward, ribs controlled, and core braced.
  - Mid: Bend the elbows and lower the dumbbell behind the head without flaring the arms wide.
  - Peak: Reach a comfortable stretch through the triceps while keeping shoulders controlled.
  - Finish: Extend the elbows to bring the dumbbell back overhead without arching the back.

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Shoulders

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use a load that lets the elbows stay mostly in place.
  - Shorten the range if elbows or shoulders get cranky.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale on the controlled lowering phase and exhale as you drive through the main effort.

**9. Tempo:** Lift with control, squeeze briefly where it counts, and lower slower than you lift.

**10. Coaching cues:**

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pressdown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Elbows point forward".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Lighter single-dumbbell extension.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** shoulders and upper back that can reach overhead comfortably.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Lighter single-dumbbell extension
  - Cable pushdown

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Lighter single-dumbbell extension

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Bench dip
  - Cross-body triceps extension
  - Overhead dumbbell extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Shoulders: stay in a pain-free range and stop if you feel any pinching.
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** Rope overhead extension is a cable triceps movement that loads the long head through an overhead angle.

**Setup:** Face away from the cable, hold the rope overhead, and set the elbows slightly in front of the head.

**Execution:** Extend the elbows until the arms straighten, keep the upper arms mostly fixed, then return under control.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Lock the upper arms in place overhead and brace so the ribs stay stacked.
  - Mid: Bend at the elbows only and lower the load behind the head under control.
  - Peak: Stop at the deepest range where the elbows stay pointed forward and the shoulders stay quiet.
  - Finish: Straighten the elbows back to the top without letting them flare wide.

## Skull crusher

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: general

**1. Exercise name:** Skull crusher

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Shoulders, Forearms

**4. Equipment:** Dumbbells or EZ-bar

**5. Difficulty:** Intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Letting elbows flare wide
  - Moving the shoulders too much
  - Lowering too fast
  - Using a range that irritates elbows
  - Letting wrists bend backward

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use light to moderate weight until elbow comfort is clear.
  - Keep the movement controlled near the head.
  - Avoid if elbows feel sharp pain.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale while lowering. Exhale while extending the elbows.

**9. Tempo:** Lower slowly for 2-3 seconds, extend with control.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Upper arms stay fixed
  - Elbows point forward
  - Lower under control

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pressdown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Upper arms stay fixed".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Slow eccentric skull crushers.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** general full-body control — no special mobility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Slow eccentric skull crushers
  - Paused reps
  - EZ-bar variation

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Overhead dumbbell extension with lighter load

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Bench dip
  - Close-grip push-up
  - Overhead dumbbell extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 8–12 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 60–90 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The skull crusher is a lying triceps extension where the elbows bend and straighten while the upper arms stay mostly fixed. It isolates the triceps more than pressing movements.

**Setup:** Lie on a bench or floor holding dumbbells or a bar above the chest. Keep upper arms angled slightly back instead of perfectly vertical, wrists neutral, and elbows pointed forward.

**Execution:** Bend the elbows and lower the weight toward the forehead or just behind the head. Keep upper arms mostly still. Extend the elbows to return the weight upward.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Lie down with the weight above the chest, elbows pointed forward, and upper arms stable.
  - Mid: Bend only the elbows and lower the weight toward the forehead or slightly behind the head.
  - Peak: Reach the bottom with control while keeping elbows from flaring out.
  - Finish: Extend the elbows and bring the weight back up without swinging the shoulders.

## Triceps pushdown

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Triceps pushdown

**2. Primary muscles:** Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Shoulders

**4. Equipment:** Machine or cable station

**5. Difficulty:** intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the torso
  - Letting elbows drift forward
  - Slamming the weight stack

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use a rope or neutral handle if elbows are sensitive.
  - Reduce range if elbow pain sharpens.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale on the controlled lowering phase and exhale as you drive through the main effort.

**9. Tempo:** Lift with control, squeeze briefly where it counts, and lower slower than you lift.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Elbows stay pinned
  - Move the forearms only
  - Quiet shoulders

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pushdown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Elbows stay pinned".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Close-grip push-up.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Close-grip push-up
  - Overhead triceps extension with lighter load

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pushdown
  - Close-grip push-up

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pressdown
  - Bench dip
  - Close-grip push-up
  - Overhead dumbbell extension

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** Triceps pushdown is a cable isolation movement that trains elbow extension without needing the shoulders to move much.

**Setup:** Stand tall at the cable, pin the elbows near the ribs, and start with the forearms bent under the handle.

**Execution:** Press the handle down until the elbows straighten, squeeze briefly, then return under control without letting the elbows drift.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand tall at the cable, pin the elbows by the ribs, and start with the forearms bent.
  - Mid: Drive the handle down until the elbows straighten without leaning over the stack.
  - Peak: Pause at lockout while the shoulders stay quiet and the triceps stay loaded.
  - Finish: Let the handle come back up slowly until the forearms bend again without the elbows drifting.
