## Band curl

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Band curl

**2. Primary muscles:** Biceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Brachialis, Forearms

**4. Equipment:** Resistance band

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Standing unevenly on the band
  - Letting elbows drift forward
  - Shrugging shoulders
  - Losing tension at the bottom
  - Letting the band snap back

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Inspect the band for damage.
  - Keep feet secure on the band.
  - Use smooth tension to protect elbows.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale while curling. Inhale while lowering.

**9. Tempo:** Curl for 1-2 seconds, lower for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Stand evenly on the band
  - Elbows stay close
  - Keep tension through the bottom

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Shorter range curl with lighter band tension.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Stand evenly on the band".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use a stronger band.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use a stronger band
  - Pause at the top
  - Slow the lowering

**15. Regressions:**
  - Shorter range curl with lighter band tension

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Biceps curl
  - Concentration curl
  - EZ-bar curl
  - Hammer curl

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Low joint stress. Stop if any sharp pain appears and make the movement smaller or easier.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The band curl trains the biceps using resistance band tension. Band resistance increases toward the top, encouraging control through the full curl.

**Setup:** Stand on the band with feet secure. Hold the band ends with arms down, palms facing forward or inward, elbows close to the body.

**Execution:** Curl the hands upward while keeping elbows mostly still. Squeeze at the top, then lower slowly while keeping tension on the band.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand securely on the band with arms down, elbows tucked, and wrists neutral.
  - Mid: Curl upward against band tension while keeping shoulders relaxed.
  - Peak: Reach the top with biceps engaged and band tension controlled.
  - Finish: Lower slowly until arms extend without letting the band snap down.

## Biceps curl

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Biceps curl

**2. Primary muscles:** Biceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Brachialis, Forearms

**4. Equipment:** Dumbbells

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the torso to lift the weight
  - Letting elbows drift too far forward
  - Shrugging the shoulders
  - Dropping the dumbbells quickly
  - Bending the wrists backward

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Choose a load you can control without momentum.
  - Keep wrists neutral and elbows comfortable.
  - Reduce load if elbow discomfort appears.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you curl up. Inhale as you lower.

**9. Tempo:** Curl for 1-2 seconds. Lower for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Elbows stay quiet
  - Curl with the arm, not the torso
  - Lower slower than you lift

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Use lighter dumbbells.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Elbows stay quiet".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use heavier dumbbells.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use heavier dumbbells
  - Slow eccentric lowering
  - Add paused reps

**15. Regressions:**
  - Use lighter dumbbells
  - Use a resistance band

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band curl
  - Concentration curl
  - EZ-bar curl
  - Hammer curl

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The biceps curl trains the front of the upper arm by bending the elbow against resistance. It is most effective when the torso stays still and the arm does the work.

**Setup:** Stand tall with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing forward or slightly inward. Keep elbows close to the ribs, shoulders relaxed, wrists neutral, and core lightly braced.

**Execution:** Curl the dumbbells upward by bending the elbows. Keep the upper arms mostly still. Squeeze briefly near the top, then lower slowly until the arms extend again.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand tall with dumbbells at your sides, elbows close to the ribs, and wrists neutral.
  - Mid: Curl the dumbbells upward without swinging the torso or letting elbows drift far forward.
  - Peak: Reach the top with biceps engaged and shoulders relaxed.
  - Finish: Lower the dumbbells slowly until the arms extend again under control.

## Hammer curl

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Hammer curl

**2. Primary muscles:** Brachialis, Brachioradialis, Biceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Forearms

**4. Equipment:** Dumbbells

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner to intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the torso to lift the weight
  - Letting elbows drift far forward
  - Bending wrists
  - Shrugging shoulders
  - Dropping the dumbbells too quickly

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use a load that does not require body momentum.
  - Keep wrists neutral to reduce forearm and elbow irritation.
  - Avoid forcing the top range if elbows feel pinched.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale while curling up. Inhale while lowering.

**9. Tempo:** Curl for 1–2 seconds, lower for 2–3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Neutral grip stays fixed
  - Elbows stay pinned
  - Lower slower than you lift

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Single-arm hammer curl.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Neutral grip stays fixed".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Heavier dumbbells.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Heavier dumbbells
  - Slow eccentric lowering
  - Cross-body hammer curl

**15. Regressions:**
  - Single-arm hammer curl
  - Resistance-band curl

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band curl
  - Biceps curl
  - Concentration curl
  - EZ-bar curl

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The hammer curl is a dumbbell curl performed with a neutral grip. It emphasizes the brachialis and brachioradialis while also training the biceps.

**Setup:** Stand tall with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing inward. Keep elbows close to the ribs, shoulders relaxed, wrists neutral, and core lightly braced.

**Execution:** Curl the dumbbells upward without swinging the torso. Keep palms facing each other and elbows mostly fixed. Lower under control until the arms are extended again.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand tall with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing inward, wrists neutral, and elbows close to the ribs.
  - Mid: Curl the dumbbells upward while keeping the upper arms still and avoiding body swing.
  - Peak: Reach the top with forearms close to vertical and biceps and forearms engaged without shrugging.
  - Finish: Lower the dumbbells slowly to the starting position while keeping control through the forearms.

## Incline dumbbell curl

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Incline dumbbell curl

**2. Primary muscles:** Biceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Brachialis, Forearms

**5. Difficulty:** Intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Using too much weight
  - Swinging elbows forward
  - Letting shoulders roll forward
  - Dropping too quickly into the stretch
  - Bending wrists

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Start light due to the increased stretch.
  - Avoid aggressive bottom range if shoulders or elbows are uncomfortable.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you curl. Inhale as you lower.

**9. Tempo:** Curl for 1-2 seconds, lower for 3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Shoulders stay back

  - Own the lowered stretch

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Standing dumbbell curl.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Shoulders stay back".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Add slow eccentrics.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Add slow eccentrics
  - Use pauses
  - Increase dumbbell load slightly

**15. Regressions:**
  - Standing dumbbell curl
  - Band curl

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band curl
  - Biceps curl
  - Concentration curl
  - EZ-bar curl

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Low joint stress. Stop if any sharp pain appears and make the movement smaller or easier.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The incline dumbbell curl is performed lying back on an incline bench. The arm starts slightly behind the torso, creating a longer biceps stretch than a standing curl.

**Setup:** Set an incline bench to a moderate angle. Lie back with dumbbells hanging at your sides. Keep shoulders back, elbows slightly behind the body, and wrists neutral.

**Execution:** Curl the dumbbells upward without swinging the upper arms forward. Squeeze near the top, then lower slowly into the stretched bottom position.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Lie back on the incline bench with arms hanging down, shoulders set, and elbows slightly behind the torso.
  - Mid: Curl the dumbbells upward while keeping upper arms quiet and wrists controlled.
  - Peak: Reach the top with biceps engaged without rolling shoulders forward.
  - Finish: Lower slowly until arms extend again and the biceps stretch under control.
