## Arnold press

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: compound

**1. Exercise name:** Arnold press

**2. Primary muscles:** Anterior deltoids, Lateral deltoids

**3. Secondary muscles:** Triceps, Upper chest, Rotator cuff stabilizers

**4. Equipment:** Dumbbells

**5. Difficulty:** Intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning back to force the press
  - Rotating too aggressively
  - Shrugging at the top
  - Lowering too fast
  - Pressing through shoulder pain

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use lighter weights than a standard shoulder press at first.
  - Keep the range pain-free and controlled.
  - Avoid if the rotation causes shoulder pinching.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale before pressing. Exhale as you press overhead.

**9. Tempo:** Press smoothly for 1–2 seconds, lower for 2–3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Rotate smoothly
  - Keep ribs stacked
  - Finish without shrugging

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Dumbbell shoulder press.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Rotate smoothly".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Add load gradually.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** shoulders and upper back that can reach overhead comfortably.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Add load gradually
  - Slow the lowering
  - Use alternating Arnold presses

**15. Regressions:**
  - Dumbbell shoulder press
  - Landmine-style press if available

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band overhead press
  - Band thruster
  - Dumbbell shoulder press
  - Dumbbell thruster

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Shoulders: stay in a pain-free range and stop if you feel any pinching.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The Arnold press is a dumbbell shoulder press variation that rotates from a palms-in front rack position into an overhead press. It increases shoulder demand and requires controlled range.

**Setup:** Sit or stand tall with dumbbells in front of the shoulders, palms facing you. Brace your core, keep ribs down, and avoid leaning back.

**Execution:** Rotate the dumbbells outward as you press overhead. Finish with palms facing forward or slightly inward. Lower with control by reversing the path back to the front rack position.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Hold dumbbells in front of your shoulders with palms facing you, elbows slightly forward, and torso braced.
  - Mid: Begin pressing while rotating the palms outward, keeping the ribs controlled.
  - Peak: Reach overhead with arms extended and shoulders stable without shrugging aggressively.
  - Finish: Lower by reversing the rotation back to the front rack position with control.

## Band lateral raise

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Band lateral raise

**2. Primary muscles:** Side delts

**3. Secondary muscles:** Upper back

**4. Equipment:** Resistance band

**5. Difficulty:** beginner

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Swinging the torso
  - Turning it into a shrug
  - Going too heavy to control the top

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Stay in a pain-free range if shoulder irritation is present.
  - Drop load before form starts to swing.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale on the controlled lowering phase and exhale as you drive through the main effort.

**9. Tempo:** Lift with control, squeeze briefly where it counts, and lower slower than you lift.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Soft elbows
  - Lead wide, not high
  - Keep the neck relaxed

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Cable raise.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Soft elbows".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Partial-range raise.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** just enough shoulder movement to reach your arms out — no special flexibility needed to start.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Partial-range raise
  - Wall slide for shoulder control work

**15. Regressions:**
  - Cable raise
  - Partial-range raise

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Cable lateral raise
  - Dumbbell lateral raise
  - Lean-away lateral raise

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Low joint stress. Stop if any sharp pain appears and make the movement smaller or easier.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** Band lateral raise is a side delts pattern matched to resistance band. used to train side delts.

**Setup:** Stand tall, keep the ribs quiet, and begin from a light but controlled start position.

**Execution:** Move the arms through the target arc without turning the rep into a shrug. Own the top range briefly while the neck stays relaxed. Lower slowly so tension stays on the target muscles instead of momentum.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand tall, keep the ribs quiet, and begin from a light but controlled start position.
  - Mid: Move the arms through the target arc without turning the rep into a shrug.
  - Peak: Own the top range briefly while the neck stays relaxed.
  - Finish: Lower slowly so tension stays on the target muscles instead of momentum.

## Band overhead press

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: compound

**1. Exercise name:** Band overhead press

**2. Primary muscles:** Anterior deltoids, Lateral deltoids, Triceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Core, Upper traps, Serratus anterior

**4. Equipment:** Resistance band

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner to intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Leaning back to finish the press
  - Letting the band pull arms down too quickly
  - Starting with uneven band tension
  - Shrugging excessively
  - Pressing through shoulder pain

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Check the band for damage before use.
  - Stand securely on the band to prevent slipping.
  - Reduce range or resistance if shoulders pinch.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you press overhead. Inhale as you lower.

**9. Tempo:** Press for 1-2 seconds, lower for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Ribs stay down
  - Press straight overhead
  - Control the band on the way down

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Seated dumbbell shoulder press with light weight.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Ribs stay down".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use a stronger band.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** shoulders and upper back that can reach overhead comfortably.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use a stronger band
  - Add a pause overhead
  - Slow the lowering

**15. Regressions:**
  - Seated dumbbell shoulder press with light weight
  - Wall slide

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Arnold press
  - Band thruster
  - Dumbbell shoulder press
  - Dumbbell thruster

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Shoulders: stay in a pain-free range and stop if you feel any pinching.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The band overhead press is a vertical pressing exercise using a resistance band. Band tension increases as the hands move upward, making control and shoulder position important.

**Setup:** Stand on the band or anchor it under the feet. Hold handles or ends at shoulder height with elbows slightly forward. Brace the core and keep ribs down.

**Execution:** Press the hands overhead until arms are extended. Keep the band path controlled and avoid leaning back. Lower slowly back to shoulder height.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Stand on the band with hands at shoulder height, ribs down, and core braced.
  - Mid: Press upward while keeping elbows controlled and avoiding a backward lean.
  - Peak: Reach overhead with arms extended and shoulders stable.
  - Finish: Lower the hands back to shoulder height under band tension.

## Cable face pull

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: compound

**1. Exercise name:** Cable face pull

**2. Primary muscles:** Rear deltoids, Rotator cuff, Upper back

**3. Secondary muscles:** Middle traps, Lower traps, Biceps

**4. Equipment:** Cable machine with rope

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner to intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Shrugging through the pull
  - Pulling too low toward the chest
  - Leaning back to finish the rep
  - Using momentum instead of upper-back control

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use a load that lets you finish with clean shoulder rotation and no neck tension.
  - Stop short of pain if the front of the shoulder feels pinchy.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you pull the rope toward the face. Inhale as you return forward.

**9. Tempo:** Pull for 1-2 seconds, pause briefly, and return for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Pull to the face
  - Elbows high, shoulders calm
  - Hands separate at the finish

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pull-apart.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Pull to the face".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Pause at peak contraction.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** the ability to pull with your back by squeezing your shoulder blades together.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Pause at peak contraction
  - Use slower return
  - Progress to single-arm face pull work

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pull-apart
  - Rear-delt cable fly
  - Scap retraction drill

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band pull-apart
  - Reverse fly
  - Reverse snow angel

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Low joint stress. Stop if any sharp pain appears and make the movement smaller or easier.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The cable face pull is a rope-based pulling exercise that finishes near face height with the elbows high and the hands separating. It trains the rear delts, upper back, and external rotators when done with control.

**Setup:** Set a rope attachment around face height. Stand tall with a stable stance, arms extended, ribs stacked, and shoulders relaxed before beginning the pull.

**Execution:** Pull the rope toward the face by driving the elbows high and back. Let the hands separate as you finish, creating external rotation at the shoulder, then return forward slowly without shrugging.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Set the rope at face height, take a stable stance, and begin with the arms extended and shoulders organized.
  - Mid: Drive the elbows back and slightly high while the rope travels toward the face.
  - Peak: Finish with the hands separated near the face and the rear delts and upper back fully engaged.
  - Finish: Return the rope forward slowly until the arms extend without losing shoulder control.
