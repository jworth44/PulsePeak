## Assisted pull-up

> 📷 Full visual sequence · Class: compound

**1. Exercise name:** Assisted pull-up

**2. Primary muscles:** Latissimus dorsi, Teres major, Biceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Rhomboids, Lower traps, Core, Forearms

**4. Equipment:** Pull-up station, Band or machine assist

**5. Difficulty:** Intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Using too much assistance and losing the real pull-up pattern
  - Shrugging the shoulders toward the ears
  - Kipping or swinging to finish the rep
  - Dropping too fast into the bottom
  - Leading only with the chin instead of the elbows

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Use enough assistance to keep the full range controlled.
  - Avoid jerking out of the bottom hang.
  - Stop if the shoulders lose position before the lats and arms fatigue.

**8. Breathing:** Inhale before pulling. Exhale as you move through the hardest part of the rep.

**9. Tempo:** Pull with intent, pause briefly at the top if controlled, and lower for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Elbows drive to the ribs
  - Shoulders stay down
  - Lower under control

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pulldown.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Elbows drive to the ribs".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Reduce assistance gradually.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** shoulders and upper back that can reach overhead comfortably.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Reduce assistance gradually
  - Pause at the top
  - Use slow eccentric lowering

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pulldown
  - Lat pulldown
  - Pull-up negative

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Back widow
  - Band pulldown
  - Chin-up
  - Lat pulldown

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Shoulders: stay in a pain-free range and stop if you feel any pinching.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The assisted pull-up is a vertical pull performed with external assistance from a band or machine. It helps build the strength and position needed for strict pull-ups while keeping the rep pattern honest.

**Setup:** Set the assistance level so you can control the full rep. Grip the bar evenly, set the shoulders down and back, brace the trunk, and start from a stable hang without excessive swinging.

**Execution:** Pull the elbows down toward the ribs and bring the chest toward the bar. Keep the shoulders controlled instead of shrugging into the ears. Lower back to the hang under control without dropping through the bottom.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Grip the bar, set the shoulders, brace the trunk, and begin from a controlled assisted hang.
  - Mid: Drive the elbows down and back while the chest rises toward the bar.
  - Peak: Reach the top with the upper back engaged and the shoulders still organized, not shrugged.
  - Finish: Lower under control back to the start and keep tension before the next rep begins.

## Back widow

> 📝 Text guide (image pending) · Class: isolation

**1. Exercise name:** Back widow

**2. Primary muscles:** Upper back, Rear deltoids, Rhomboids

**3. Secondary muscles:** Triceps, Core

**4. Equipment:** Bodyweight

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner to intermediate

**6. Common mistakes:**
  - Cranking the neck backward to create the lift
  - Shrugging the shoulders into the ears
  - Bridging from the hips instead of lifting from the upper back
  - Dropping back to the floor with no control
  - Placing the elbows too far away from the torso

**7. Safety warnings:**
  - Keep the neck long and stop if the front of the shoulder feels pinchy.
  - Use a smaller lift if you cannot keep the tension in the upper back.
  - Move slowly enough to separate upper-back work from momentum.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you lift. Inhale as you lower.

**9. Tempo:** Lift for 1-2 seconds, hold briefly at the top, and lower for 2-3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**
  - Drive through the elbows
  - Lift the chest not the chin
  - Lower softly

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Band pull-apart.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Drive through the elbows".
  - Use light or no weight until the movement feels smooth and repeatable, then add weight slowly.
  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Hold longer at the top.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** the ability to pull with your back by squeezing your shoulder blades together.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Hold longer at the top
  - Use slower lowering
  - Progress to band or dumbbell rows

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band pull-apart
  - Chest-supported row
  - Scap retraction drill

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Assisted pull-up
  - Band pulldown
  - Chin-up
  - Lat pulldown

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Elbows/wrists: move smoothly and don't jerk or fully snap the joint straight.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 10–15 reps

**19. Ideal rest period:** 45–60 sec

**20. Training purpose:** The back widow is a bodyweight upper-back drill where you drive through the elbows to lift the chest and upper torso. It helps train the upper back without needing a row station or pull-up bar.

**Setup:** Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Place the elbows on the floor beside the torso, brace lightly through the trunk, and keep the neck long.

**Execution:** Drive the elbows into the floor to lift the chest and upper back. Focus on squeezing the mid-back rather than arching the neck. Lower back down softly and repeat with control.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Set the elbows by the sides, feet planted, trunk lightly braced, and neck long.
  - Mid: Drive through the elbows to lift the chest and upper back off the floor.
  - Peak: Pause at the top with the upper back engaged and the shoulders away from the ears.
  - Finish: Lower back down softly without collapsing or letting the neck take over.

## Band pulldown

**1. Exercise name:** Band pulldown

**2. Primary muscles:** Latissimus dorsi, Teres major, Biceps

**3. Secondary muscles:** Rear deltoids, Lower traps, Core

**5. Difficulty:** Beginner

  - Avoid yanking from the top position.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you pull down. Inhale as you return.

**9. Tempo:** Pull for 1–2 seconds, return for 2–3 seconds.

**10. Coaching cues:**

**11. Beginner tips:**
  - If it feels hard, start with an easier version: Straight-arm band pulldown with lighter tension.
  - Focus on one thing first: "Pull elbows to ribs".

  - Move slowly and watch yourself in a mirror or video to check your positions.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use a stronger band.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** shoulders and upper back that can reach overhead comfortably.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use a stronger band
  - Slow the return
  - Pause at the bottom

**15. Regressions:**
  - Straight-arm band pulldown with lighter tension

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Assisted pull-up
  - Back widow
  - Chin-up
  - Lat pulldown

**17. Injury considerations:**
  - Shoulders: stay in a pain-free range and stop if you feel any pinching.

**18. Ideal rep range:** 6–10 reps (strength: 4–6 · muscle: 8–12)

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The band pulldown is a vertical pulling exercise using a resistance band anchored overhead. It trains the lats and upper back while allowing a controlled pulling path.

**Setup:** Anchor the band securely overhead. Kneel or stand facing the anchor, hold the band with arms extended, ribs down, and shoulders relaxed away from ears.

**Execution:** Pull the elbows down toward the ribs while keeping the chest tall and shoulders controlled. Pause briefly, then let the arms return overhead slowly.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Set the band overhead, arms extended, ribs down, and shoulders controlled.
  - Mid: Pull elbows down and slightly back, aiming to bring them toward the sides of the ribs.
  - Peak: Reach the strongest contraction with lats engaged and shoulders away from the ears.
  - Finish: Return the arms overhead slowly while keeping tension and control.

**10. Coaching cues:**

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Use a stronger band.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.

**13. Mobility requirements:** the ability to pull with your back by squeezing your shoulder blades together.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Use a stronger band
  - Pause longer at full row
  - Slow the eccentric return

**15. Regressions:**
  - Doorframe row
  - Chest-supported row
  - Light seated band row

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band row to squat
  - Bear crawl drag
  - Chest-supported row
  - Doorframe row

**17. Injury considerations:**

**19. Ideal rest period:** 90–180 sec (heavier = longer)

**20. Training purpose:** The band row is a horizontal pulling exercise performed against band tension. It trains the lats, rhomboids, and middle back while staying easy to set up at home or in a small space.

**Setup:** Anchor the band in front of you around chest or stomach height. Stand or sit in a stable position with the arms extended, ribs stacked, and shoulders relaxed away from the ears.

**Execution:** Pull the elbows back toward the lower ribs while keeping the torso quiet and the chest organized. Squeeze the upper back at the end of the row, then return slowly until the arms are extended again.

**Visual sequence (steps):**
  - Start: Set the anchor, extend the arms, and organize the ribs and shoulders before the pull.
  - Mid: Drive the elbows back while the torso stays quiet and the wrists remain neutral.
  - Peak: Squeeze the mid-back and lats with the hands near the lower ribs and shoulders still down.
  - Finish: Let the arms extend slowly until the band stays taut and the shoulders remain controlled.

**8. Breathing:** Exhale as you row. Inhale as you lower.

**9. Tempo:** Row up smoothly, pause briefly at the top, and lower for 2-3 seconds.

**12. Advanced tips:**
  - Make it harder: Increase load gradually.
  - Add tempo — slow the lowering part or add a pause to make it harder without more weight.
  - Once form is automatic, add small amounts of weight or a rep or two week to week.

**13. Mobility requirements:** the ability to pull with your back by squeezing your shoulder blades together.

**14. Progressions:**
  - Increase load gradually
  - Pause at peak contraction
  - Use tempo rows with slower lowering

**15. Regressions:**
  - Band row
  - Doorframe row

**16. Alternative exercises:**
  - Band row

**Visual sequence (steps):**

  - Peak: Pause at the top with the mid-back and lats engaged without shrugging or lifting the chest.
  - Finish: Lower the dumbbells under control until the arms extend and the shoulders stay organized.
