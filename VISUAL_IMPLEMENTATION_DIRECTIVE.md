# PulsePeak — Visual Implementation Directive

**Status: APPROVED (owner, 2026-07-07). Creative Direction V2 is ratified.**
This directive is the governing visual-implementation authority. It elaborates —
does not replace — `CREATIVE_DIRECTION_V2.md`. Where an engineering constraint
forces adaptation, preserve the *experience*, not the pixels.

> Reproduce the experience, not the pixels. The finished application should make
> users feel exactly the way the approved concept boards do. Concept boards
> define **composition, typography, spacing, emotional tone, hierarchy,
> interaction** — not exact pixel placement. Do not chase realism in the mockups.

---

## Overall feel

PulsePeak must **no longer resemble** an admin dashboard, enterprise software, a
developer tool, a database front-end, or a collection of widgets.

It must feel like an **editorial product** — a premium fitness publication, a
luxury coaching experience: calm, intentional, confident, trustworthy,
beautifully restrained. Every screen must say **"this product was designed,"**
never "this product was assembled."

**Personality:** warm · human · athletic · confident · premium · editorial.
Never cold, clinical, overly technical, corporate, gamer-like, or futuristic for
its own sake.

## Layout philosophy — story-first, not dashboard-first

Every screen has **one primary story, one primary hero, one primary action.**
Everything else supports it. The eye travels naturally; nothing competes.

- **Desktop** = the mobile experience with more breathing room. Centered content
  column, generous outer margins, reduced noise, quiet/floating top nav,
  restrained chrome, cinematic spacing. Calm, not dense.
- **Mobile** is the primary design target: one-handed comfort, thumb reach,
  clarity, focus, speed. **Desktop inherits from mobile — never the reverse.**

## Typography — hierarchy from type, not borders

Typography is a major part of the brand. Large headlines feel editorial; body
copy warm and readable; small labels quiet; numbers deliberate. Confidence
without shouting. Use type to create hierarchy **instead of borders**.

## Spacing — rhythm

Increase whitespace; reduce clutter; every major section breathes. Nothing
compressed, nothing randomly spaced. Spacing creates rhythm.

## Card system — merge, don't multiply

The app leans too hard on cards. Cards become **larger, simpler, cleaner, more
intentional.** Every card earns its place. **If multiple cards can become one
experience — merge them.**

## Color — meaning, not decoration; two identities

Color communicates meaning, not decoration.

- **Daylight** — warm paper · deep pine · warm espresso text. Organic, editorial.
- **Midnight** — warm graphite · ember · warm cream text. Comfortable, never harsh.

The two themes are **two premium identities — not one palette inverted, and with
NO shared accent colors.** Brand recognition comes from **layout, type,
composition, voice, motion, honesty — not color.**

## Motion

Calm, purposeful, physical, subtle. Never flashy or distracting. Every animation
reinforces understanding.

## Voice — an experienced coach

Speaks like a coach, not software/AI/a dashboard/a robot. Personal, helpful,
supportive, honest. **Never overstate certainty; never fabricate insight.**

## Media — one premium production

Every photograph must feel like one premium commercial shoot. Review lighting,
composition, crop, model consistency, background, camera height, lens feel, color
grading, exercise form, sequence continuity. Nothing looks stitched together from
unrelated productions.

- **Exercise cards** stop resembling database records: reduce visible metadata,
  increase visual storytelling. **The image teaches first; the title confirms
  second; supporting info appears only when helpful.** Invite exploration, not
  documentation.
- **Movement guides** teach, don't overwhelm: **Start · Move · Peak · Finish** —
  like flipping through a premium coaching manual, not technical docs.

## Navigation

Navigation disappears into the background. It supports movement through the
product; it never becomes the product.

## Visual restraint (the core discipline)

Do not decorate. Do not add effects because they look modern. Every visual
element must justify its existence. **Remove visual noise relentlessly. Premium
products are remembered for what they remove, not what they add.**

---

## Implementation standard

Do not recreate screenshots — recreate the experience. When constraints require
adaptation, **preserve: composition · hierarchy · visual rhythm · typography ·
spacing · interaction · emotion · honesty · craftsmanship.**

This directive governs every capability in `PRODUCTION_ROADMAP.md` (C1–C12).
Each screen closes only when it meets this feel in **both flagship identities
(Daylight + Midnight)** and the Product Excellence Standard's Definition of Done.
